# Common AI Mistakes

AI can be helpful, but it can also:

- make up facts
- sound too confident
- miss important context
- give generic answers
- carry the wrong context forward in long chats

This does not mean AI is useless. It means you should use it with awareness.

The biggest beginner mistake is assuming that a polished answer must be a correct answer. Sadly, smooth writing and real accuracy are not the same thing.

## 1. Making Up Facts

AI can invent:

- quotes
- statistics
- sources
- names
- product details
- events that never happened

This is sometimes called a hallucination.

## 2. Sounding Too Sure

AI often writes in a confident tone, even when it is uncertain.

That can make wrong information feel believable.

## 3. Missing Important Context

If your prompt is vague, the AI may fill in the gaps with assumptions.

That can lead to answers that sound reasonable but do not actually match your situation.

## 4. Giving Generic Advice

Without enough detail, AI may give bland answers like:

> Improve your marketing by posting more online.

That is not technically wrong, but it is not very useful either.

## 5. Carrying Old Context Forward

In long chats, the AI can keep reusing earlier information even when your goal has changed.

That is why a new chat often helps.

## 6. Struggling With High-Stakes Topics

Be extra careful with:

- medical advice
- legal guidance
- taxes
- financial decisions
- safety-related instructions

AI can help you prepare questions or summarize information, but it should not replace a qualified expert.

## Be Careful With

- medical advice
- legal topics
- taxes
- financial decisions

Always review and verify important outputs.

## How to Protect Yourself

- verify important facts
- ask for sources when relevant
- use clearer prompts
- start a new chat if the context becomes messy
- do not trust confidence alone

## Red Flags

Slow down and double-check if:

- the answer sounds very certain but gives no evidence
- the AI names a source you cannot verify
- the advice feels too broad or too absolute
- the output is about health, law, taxes, or money

## Best Mindset

Use AI as a helpful assistant, not a final authority.

It is great for drafting, explaining, organizing, and brainstorming. It is not perfect judgment in a box.
