# Using AI Safely

Do not paste sensitive information into AI tools unless you understand the privacy risks.

AI is powerful, but safety matters just as much as convenience.

The safest approach is to treat every prompt like something you may not want to share casually if it contains personal, financial, medical, or confidential details.

Avoid sharing:

- passwords
- bank details
- private customer data
- confidential documents
- sensitive personal records

Also be careful with:

- private contracts
- internal company strategy
- private school records
- identity numbers
- anything covered by confidentiality rules

Use AI as support for drafting, summarizing, and brainstorming — not as your final authority for high-stakes decisions.

## Before You Paste Something Into AI

Ask yourself:

- does this include private information?
- does this belong to a customer, client, patient, or employer?
- would I be comfortable sharing this publicly?
- can I remove names, numbers, or identifying details first?

If the answer makes you hesitate, clean the information before using it.

## Be Careful With High-Stakes Advice

Do not rely on AI alone for:

- medical decisions
- legal decisions
- tax filing
- investment advice
- safety-critical steps

AI can help you understand a topic or prepare questions, but it should not be your only source.

## Watch for Confident Errors

AI may sound polished and certain even when it is wrong.

That is why it is smart to:

- verify important claims
- cross-check facts
- review messages before sending them
- ask for sources when accuracy matters

## Safe Ways to Use AI

AI is often most useful for:

- drafting emails
- summarizing notes
- brainstorming ideas
- simplifying explanations
- organizing plans and checklists

Those are lower-risk tasks than asking it to make major decisions for you.

## Good Safety Habits

- remove private details before pasting text
- keep prompts focused and necessary
- start a new chat instead of dragging sensitive context forward
- review important outputs carefully
- use your own judgment before acting

## Quick Rule

> If it is private, personal, regulated, or high-stakes, slow down and review before using AI.
