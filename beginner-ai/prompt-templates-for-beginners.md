# Prompt Templates for Beginners

Copy, paste, and customize these.

These templates are meant to save time. Replace the bracketed parts with your real details, remove what you do not need, and keep the final prompt simple.

## Email

> Help me write a **[type of email]**. The situation is **[context]**. The tone should be **[tone]**. Keep it under **[length]**.

Example:

> Help me write a follow-up email after a job interview. The tone should be warm and professional. Keep it under 120 words.

## Rewrite

> Rewrite this so it sounds **[clearer / simpler / more professional]**: **[text]**

Example:

> Rewrite this so it sounds more professional: "Hi, just checking if you saw my last message."

## Summary

> Summarize this in **[3 bullet points / plain English / short paragraphs]**: **[text]**

Example:

> Summarize this article in plain English and give me 5 bullet points.

## Study help

> Explain **[topic]** to a beginner. Use simple language, one example, and 3 quiz questions.

Example:

> Explain photosynthesis to a beginner. Use simple language, one example, and 3 quiz questions.

## Resume help

> Improve these resume bullet points so they sound clearer and stronger, but keep them honest and realistic: **[paste bullet points]**

Example:

> Improve these resume bullet points so they sound clearer and stronger, but keep them honest and realistic: "Helped customers, answered calls, organized files."

## Small business

> I run a **[type of business]**. Give me **[number]** content ideas for **[channel]**. Keep them simple and practical.

Example:

> I run a local coffee shop. Give me 10 content ideas for Instagram. Keep them simple and practical.

## Planning

> Create a simple plan for **[goal]**. I have **[time / budget / limits]**. Put the answer in a step-by-step list.

Example:

> Create a simple plan for learning basic Excel. I have 30 minutes a day for one week. Put the answer in a step-by-step list.

## Comparison

> Compare **[option A]** and **[option B]** for **[goal]**. Use a table with pros, cons, and who each option is best for.

Example:

> Compare learning with YouTube and learning with an online course for basic graphic design. Use a table with pros, cons, and who each option is best for.

## Brainstorming

> Give me **[number]** ideas for **[topic]**. I want the style to feel **[tone or style]**.

Example:

> Give me 15 name ideas for a handmade candle brand. I want the style to feel warm, modern, and cozy.

## New chat handoff

> I am starting a new chat. Here is the context: **[summary]**. My goal is **[goal]**. Please continue by **[task]**.

Example:

> I am starting a new chat. Here is the context: I run a small bakery and I am preparing for a Mother's Day campaign. My goal is to create social content. Please continue by giving me 5 Instagram captions and 3 email subject lines.

## Tips for Using Templates

- keep only the parts you need
- add context when the task is specific
- ask for tone and format when it matters
- if the answer is weak, ask for a revision instead of rewriting from scratch

Templates are a starting point, not a cage. Trim them down when a task is simple.
