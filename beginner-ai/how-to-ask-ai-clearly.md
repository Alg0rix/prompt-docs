# How to Ask AI Clearly

Better prompts usually come from clarity, not complexity.

You do not need special magic words. You just need to tell the AI what you want, what it should know, and what the answer should look like.

## The 3 Things to Include

1. what you want
2. the context
3. the format or tone

Template:

> Help me with **[task]**. The context is **[details]**. Please respond in **[format or tone]**.

That simple structure is enough for many everyday tasks.

## Vague vs Clear

Too vague:

> Write something about marketing.

Better:

> I run a small bakery. Give me 5 simple Instagram marketing ideas for Mother's Day. Keep them low-cost and easy to do this week.

Why the second one works better:

- it explains the business type
- it explains the goal
- it gives a channel
- it asks for a number of ideas
- it adds a real-world constraint

Clarity removes guesswork.

## Add Context That Matters

Context helps the AI understand your situation.

Useful context can include:

- who you are
- who the answer is for
- what you are trying to achieve
- any limits like time, budget, or tone

Example:

> Help me create a weekly workout plan.

Better:

> Help me create a weekly workout plan. I am a beginner, I only have 20 minutes a day, and I want something simple I can do at home.

## Ask for the Format You Want

If you do not ask for a format, the AI may choose one for you.

That is not always bad, but asking for a format often makes the answer much easier to use.

Examples:

- "Put the answer in bullet points."
- "Give me a table with pros and cons."
- "Write this as a short email."
- "Give me 3 versions."
- "Keep it under 100 words."

## Ask for the Tone You Want

Tone changes how the answer feels.

Examples:

- friendly
- professional
- formal
- casual
- simple
- persuasive

Example:

> Write a short message to a customer about a delayed order. Keep it friendly and professional.

## Easy Upgrades

- say who the answer is for
- ask for bullet points, a table, or a short email
- ask for a friendly, simple, or professional tone
- add word or length limits

Here are a few simple prompt upgrades:

### Basic

> Explain budgeting.

### Better

> Explain budgeting to a complete beginner. Use simple language and one real-life example.

### Basic

> Help me write a summary.

### Better

> Help me write a summary of this meeting. Focus on the decisions made and end with 3 action items.

## Fix Weak Answers

If the first answer is weak, try:

- make it shorter
- use simpler language
- give me 3 options
- turn it into bullet points
- add an example

You can also say:

- "Use simpler language."
- "Make it sound less formal."
- "Give me a version for beginners."
- "Focus only on the most important parts."

That is normal. Good prompting is often a short back-and-forth process.

## Quick Checklist Before You Send a Prompt

Ask yourself:

- Did I say what I want?
- Did I include the important background?
- Did I ask for the right format?
- Did I say who the answer is for?
- Did I mention any limits?

If the answer is yes to most of those, your prompt is probably strong enough.
