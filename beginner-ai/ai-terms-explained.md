# AI Terms Explained

This glossary explains common AI words in simple language.

You do not need to memorize all of them. The goal is just to make AI tools feel less confusing.

## AI

Software that can help with tasks like writing, summarizing, and answering questions.

In everyday use, this often means a chat tool that can help you think, write, plan, or explain.

## LLM

A large language model trained on lots of text.

This is the kind of AI behind many chat assistants.

## Prompt

The instruction or question you give to the AI.

Example: "Summarize this article in 5 bullet points."

## Context

The background information the AI needs.

Example: who the answer is for, what situation you are in, and what tone you want.

## Chat Session

One conversation with the AI where earlier messages can affect later answers.

That is why old context can help sometimes and hurt sometimes.

## Hallucination

When AI makes something up and presents it like a fact.

This can include invented quotes, fake references, or incorrect facts.

## Tone

The style or feeling of the answer, such as friendly, formal, simple, or professional.

## Output Format

How you want the answer organized, such as a paragraph, list, table, checklist, or email draft.

## Multimodal

AI that can work with more than one kind of input, such as text and images.

## Token

A small unit of text used internally by AI systems.

You do not need to worry about tokens much as a beginner, but they affect how much text can fit into one interaction.

## Fine-Tuning

A more advanced way of adapting an AI model for a specific type of task or data.

## Prompt Engineering

The process of writing and refining prompts so AI gives better results.

For beginners, this often just means being clearer, adding context, and asking for the right format.

## Why These Terms Matter

You do not need to sound technical to use AI well.

But understanding a few basic words helps you:

- ask better questions
- understand why outputs change
- notice common mistakes
- use tools with more confidence
