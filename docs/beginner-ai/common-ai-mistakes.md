# Common AI Mistakes

AI can be very useful, but it is not perfect.

Sometimes it sounds polished and confident even when the answer is incomplete, misleading, or wrong.

## 1. Making Up Facts

AI may invent:

- statistics
- quotes
- sources
- product details
- historical facts

This is one reason you should verify important information.

## 2. Sounding More Certain Than It Should

AI often presents answers in a smooth, confident style.

That does **not** always mean the answer is reliable.

## 3. Missing Important Context

If your prompt is vague, the AI may fill in missing details with assumptions.

That can lead to answers that sound reasonable but miss your real goal.

## 4. Giving Generic Answers

Without enough detail, AI may produce advice that is too broad to be useful.

Example:

> "Improve your marketing by posting more online."

That is not wrong, but it is not very helpful either.

## 5. Carrying Wrong Context Forward

In a long chat, the AI may keep using old instructions even after your goal has changed.

This is one reason a fresh chat can help.

## 6. Struggling With High-Stakes Topics

Be extra careful with:

- medical advice
- legal guidance
- taxes
- financial decisions
- safety-critical information

AI can support your thinking, but it should not replace a qualified professional.

## How to Protect Yourself

- double-check important facts
- ask for sources when relevant
- use clearer prompts
- start a new chat when context gets messy
- do not trust confident wording by itself

## A Good Mindset

Use AI as a helpful draft partner, not as an automatic truth machine.
