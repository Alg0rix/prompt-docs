# How to Ask AI Clearly

Many weak AI results come from one simple problem: the request is too vague.

The good news is that you do not need advanced prompt engineering to fix that. You usually just need to be clearer.

## The Simple Formula

When asking AI for help, include:

1. **The task** — what you want done
2. **The context** — what the AI should know
3. **The format** — how you want the answer

Simple template:

> Help me with **[task]**. The context is **[details]**. Please respond in **[format or tone]**.

Example:

> Help me write a short email to a customer who asked for a refund. The tone should be polite and calm. Keep it under 120 words.

## Vague vs Clear

### Too vague

> Write something about marketing.

### Better

> I run a small bakery. Give me 5 simple Instagram marketing ideas for Mother's Day. Keep them low-cost and easy to do this week.

The second version works better because it tells the AI:

- who you are
- what kind of business you have
- what topic matters
- how many ideas you want
- what constraints you have

## Small Changes That Improve Results

### Say who the answer is for

> Explain this to a beginner.

> Rewrite this for customers, not technical staff.

### Ask for the format you want

> Put the answer in bullet points.

> Give me a table with pros and cons.

> Write 3 short versions.

### Ask for the tone you want

> Make it friendly and professional.

> Keep the tone simple and warm.

### Add limits

> Keep it under 100 words.

> Use simple language.

> Avoid jargon.

## Easy Prompt Upgrades

You can improve many prompts by adding one more sentence.

### Basic

> Help me write a meeting summary.

### Better

> Help me write a meeting summary. The meeting was about project delays and next steps. Keep it short and professional, with 3 action items at the end.

### Basic

> Explain budgeting.

### Better

> Explain budgeting to a complete beginner. Use plain language and give one real-life example.

## If the First Answer Is Not Great

That is normal. You do not need to start over right away.

Try follow-ups like:

- "Make it simpler."
- "Give me 3 options."
- "Make it shorter."
- "Use a more professional tone."
- "Add an example."
- "Turn this into bullet points."

## A Good Habit

Before sending a prompt, ask yourself:

- What do I want?
- What does the AI need to know?
- What should the final answer look like?

If you answer those three questions, your prompt is usually strong enough.
