# Using AI Safely

AI tools can be useful, but you should be careful about what you share and what you trust.

## Do Not Share Sensitive Information

Avoid pasting:

- passwords
- bank details
- private customer data
- confidential company information
- personal identity numbers
- medical records unless you fully understand the privacy risk

If possible, remove names, account numbers, and other sensitive details before using AI.

## Be Careful With High-Stakes Advice

Do not rely on AI alone for:

- legal decisions
- tax advice
- medical decisions
- financial planning

AI can help you prepare questions or summarize information, but it should not be your only source.

## Watch for Confident Errors

AI may sound very sure even when it is wrong.

That is why it is smart to:

- verify facts
- cross-check important claims
- review outputs before sending or publishing them

## Use AI as Support, Not Replacement

AI is strongest when it helps you:

- draft
- summarize
- brainstorm
- simplify
- organize

You should still make the final judgment.

## Safe Beginner Habits

- keep prompts clear and focused
- start a new chat when context gets messy
- avoid uploading private files unless necessary
- review important outputs carefully
- ask for plain-language explanations when something seems unclear
