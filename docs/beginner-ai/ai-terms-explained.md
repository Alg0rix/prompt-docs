# AI Terms Explained

This glossary explains common AI terms in simple language.

## AI

**Artificial Intelligence** is software that can perform tasks such as writing, summarizing, answering questions, or recognizing patterns.

## LLM

**Large Language Model** is a type of AI trained on large amounts of text so it can generate and understand language.

## Prompt

A **prompt** is the instruction or question you give to the AI.

## Context

**Context** is the background information the AI needs in order to give a better answer.

## Chat Session

A **chat session** is one conversation with the AI. Earlier messages in the same session can affect later answers.

## Hallucination

A **hallucination** happens when AI makes something up and presents it as if it were true.

## Tone

**Tone** is the style or feeling of the answer, such as friendly, formal, professional, or casual.

## Output Format

The **output format** is how you want the answer organized, such as a list, table, paragraph, or email draft.

## Multimodal

**Multimodal** means the AI can work with more than one kind of input, such as text and images.

## Fine-Tuning

**Fine-tuning** is a more advanced way of adjusting an AI model for a specific kind of task or data.

## Token

A **token** is a small chunk of text used internally by AI systems when processing language.

You do not usually need to think about tokens as a beginner, but they affect how much information can fit into one interaction.
